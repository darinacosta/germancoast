The Swamps & Refineries of Southwestern Lake Pontchartrain

Louisiana's German Coast is a complex mosaic of denuded swamplands, oil infrastructure, and industrial skylines. This page hosts a short series of interrelated multi-media vignettes that provide a tour of the region and emphasize its unique geographic features. Please begin by observing a few of the broader characteristics of the River Parish landscape: the stark divide between swamp and suburb along the St. Charles/Jefferson parish line; saltwater erosion along the shores of the lake; residential areas sandwiched between refineries; others that extended into precarious floodplains; and the telltale radial canals of cypress logging.

LaBranche

The cypress swamps and marsh prairies of the LaBranche Wetlands have so far survived the canal-dredging, erosion, and speculative real-estate ventures that have consumed neighboring ecosystems.
