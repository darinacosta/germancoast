The German Coast

Geographies of Southwestern Lake Pontchartrain & Beyond

The southwestern rim of Louisiana's Lake Pontchartrain is a complex mosaic of denuded swamplands, oil infrastructure, and highway sprawl. This application hosts a series of interrelated multi-media vignettes that provide a tour of the region and emphasize its unique geographic features.

As you make your way through the app, you'll come across highlighted links. Click on these to trigger events in the application, such as adding layers to the map, revealing images, or opening pop-ups.

Please begin by observing a few of the broader characteristics of the River Parish landscape, such as the stark divide between swamp and suburb along the St. Charles/Jefferson parish line; saltwater erosion along the shores of the lake; residential areas that extend into precarious floodplains; and the telltale radial canals of cypress logging.

Development Notes

This application is a work in progress, and is best viewed in Google Chrome or Firefox. Support for mobile devices is limited, but improvements are coming soon. Not all vignettes are available, and those that are may not be complete. Refer to the "Updates" section for production notes, and check back regularly. We're adding new features and content all the time.

The West Indian Hurricane of 1915

The West Indian Hurricane of 1915 followed a virtually identical course to that of Hurricane Isaac (2012). Click play to begin a multi-media exploration of these catastrophic storms, narrated by longtime river parish resident Donald Tregre.
